üìâ Low-Pass Filters (LPF1 & LPF2)

Low-pass filters remove high-frequency noise from the gyro signal. RotorFlight supports multiple filter types including PT1, PT2, PT3, Butterworth, Bessel, and Damped filters. Each has different characteristics:
  • PT1/PT2/PT3: Simple filters with increasing order. Higher orders provide more attenuation but more phase delay.
  • Butterworth: Maximally flat frequency response in the passband, good for general noise reduction.
  • Bessel: Linear phase response, preserves signal shape better but less attenuation.
  • Damped: Critically damped response, good compromise between attenuation and phase delay.

üîΩ Static Notch Filters

Static notch filters remove specific frequency components from the signal. They are useful for eliminating known sources of vibration or interference at fixed frequencies. Unlike dynamic notch filters, these have fixed center frequencies and are typically used to remove:
  • Rotor vibrations: Fixed frequency vibrations from specific motor/rotor combinations
  • Frame resonances: Natural vibration frequencies of the helicopter frame
  • Electrical interference: Noise from ESCs, servos, or other electronics
The Q value determines how narrow and deep the notch is - higher Q means a narrower, deeper notch.

üìä Dynamic Notch Filter (FFT-based)

Dynamic notch filters use FFT analysis to automatically detect and filter out dominant frequency components in the gyro signal. They adapt in real-time to changing vibration patterns. This is particularly useful for:
  • Variable RPM rotors: As throttle and collective pitch change, rotor vibration frequencies change
  • Flight conditions: Different flight modes (hover, forward flight, aerobatics) may introduce different vibration patterns
  • Automatic tuning: No manual frequency setting required
The system continuously analyzes the gyro signal, finds the strongest frequency components, and places notch filters at those frequencies.
